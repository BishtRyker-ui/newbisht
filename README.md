CI/CD stands for Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery (or Deployment).Think of it like an automatic factory assembly line for building video games or apps. Instead of humans manually checking every single piece of code, testing it, and putting it on the internet, a robot system does it automatically every time a change is made.🟢 Part 1: Continuous Integration (CI)The "Putting Pieces Together" RobotImagine you and three friends are building a giant LEGO castle, but you are all building your parts in different rooms.Without CI: You bring all your pieces together at the very end. Nothing fits. The doors are too big, and the walls are the wrong color. You spend days fixing it. This is called "integration hell."With CI: Every time you finish building just one small brick, a robot instantly grabs it, checks if it fits perfectly with your friends' bricks, and runs tests to make sure it won't fall over. If it breaks, the robot sounds an alarm so you can fix it immediately.🔵 Part 2: Continuous Delivery & Deployment (CD)The "Delivery Truck" RobotOnce the LEGO castle is perfectly built and tested, it needs to get to the store so people can play with it.Continuous Delivery: The robot packages the finished castle nicely, puts it in the delivery truck, and drives it to the store's parking lot. It waits for you to push a button to say, "Okay, put it on the shelves!"Continuous Deployment: The robot doesn't wait for you. It safely drives to the store, walks inside, and puts the toy right on the shelf for users to buy instantly.
